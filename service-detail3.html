MEP Drawings Submission & Approval Process in Qatar

1. Initial Meeting with Client
Discuss client requirements, building usage, systems needed (HVAC, lighting, water, etc.), and budget.
↓
2. Review of Architectural Drawings
Study the architectural plans to understand the layout, spaces, and coordination needs.
↓
3. Preliminary MEP Design Proposal
Prepare a basic MEP design concept considering regulatory requirements and building functionality.
↓
4. Client Feedback & Concept Revisions
Present the initial concept to the client, receive feedback, and revise accordingly.
↓
5. Detailed MEP Drawings Development
Prepare comprehensive MEP drawings, including: • HVAC system layouts • Lighting and power plans • Plumbing and drainage systems • Fire fighting and fire alarm layouts
↓
6.Internal Coordination with Architect & Structural Engineer
Check for clashes, space constraints, and ensure full alignment between disciplines.
↓
7. Pre-Submission Compliance Check
Verify drawings meet Qatar Civil Defense (QCDD), Kahramaa, Ashghal, and Baladiya requirements.
↓
8. Submission to Municipality / Relevant Authority
Submit to the local Baladiya or Online Building Permit System (OBPS) along with architectural and structural drawings.
↓
9.Municipality Review & Feedback
Authorities review MEP drawings for compliance with codes and regulations.
↓
10.Comments / Required Modifications
If comments or rejections are issued: • Analyze feedback • Discuss with client if major changes are needed
↓
11.Drawings Revision & Correction
Modify MEP drawings based on municipality comments, ensuring accuracy and compliance.
↓
12. Resubmission of Revised Drawings
Submit the corrected drawings for a second review.
↓
13. Final Approval of MEP Drawings
Once approved, drawings are cleared for use.
↓
14. Final Approval of Building Permit
After all (architectural, structural, and MEP) disciplines are approved, the building permit is issued, allowing construction to begin.

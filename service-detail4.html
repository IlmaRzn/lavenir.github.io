Architectural Drawings Submission & Approval Process in Qatar

1. Initial Client Meeting
Discuss project requirements, budget, timeline, and vision.
Understand client expectations and project goals.
↓
2. Site Analysis & Feasibility Study
Survey the site (topography, legal zoning, utilities).
Review Ashghal, Baladiya, and other authority regulations.
Assess any restrictions or challenges.
↓
3. Conceptual Design Preparation
Prepare initial design concepts and layout options.
Ensure compliance with Qatar Construction Specifications (QCS) and municipal guidelines.
↓
4. Client Feedback & Revisions
Present concept to client.
Collect feedback and modify the design accordingly.
↓
5. Detailed Architectural Drawings Development
Develop full architectural plans (floor plans, elevations, sections).
Ensure integration of design changes requested by the client.
↓
6. Coordination with Structural & MEP Engineers
Structural engineer designs foundations, slabs, beams, etc.
MEP engineer prepares electrical, plumbing, HVAC layouts.
Full coordination to ensure consistency and avoid clashes.
↓
7. Submission to Municipality (Baladiya)
Submit architectural, structural, and MEP drawings.
Include all necessary documents: land ownership, client ID, title deed, zoning approvals, etc.
↓
8. Municipality Review
Baladiya reviews the submission.
Checks for compliance with planning, safety, and regulation standards.
↓
9. Comments & Required Modifications (if any)
Municipality may return comments or rejection.
Could involve zoning issues, setback violations, or documentation errors.
↓
10. Revision of Drawings
Address comments provided by the municipality.
Modify architectural, structural, or MEP drawings accordingly.
↓
11. Resubmission to Municipality
Submit revised set of drawings.
Wait for further review and clearance.
↓
12. Final Approval of Building Permit
Once approved, the building permit is issued.
Construction can legally begin.
